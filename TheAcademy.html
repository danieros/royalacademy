Education at

Royal Academy is a privately owned, registered Impak Tutor Centre in terms of Section 51 of the South African Schools Act (Act 84 of 1996). We are located at 67 Glen Luce Drive, Douglasdale, Johannesburg.

Royal Learning Academy utilizes the Impak curriculum, which is in line with the National Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statement (CAPS). This implies that all pupils can transfer to mainstream schools whenever they want. Our environment is perfect for students who do not cope well in mainstream schools or who prefer to learn in small groups. We also find that kids who have been in environments where they have been bullied, they do better in a smaller environment. The small environment can be monitored more closely and the learners are taught how to treat each other with respect and not bully anybody.

Assessments:

On-going assessments are done by way of work or performances in class using various checklists, test, daily observations and portfolio work. All students will keep a portfolio of work done. The marks for these assessments and tasks completed are sent to Impak who then compile the report.

Language:

Royal Academy learners need to proficient in English or Afrikaans.

Daily Routine:

Our day runs from 08h00 – 13h00 Monday to Friday. Lunch and snacks will be provided. Staff will be on hand daily from 7:30 for drop-off.

After Care:

Aftercare is available, snack will be provided. Aftercare closes at 17:30. Late collection penalty will be applicable unless prior arrangements have been made.

Holiday care will be available for March and September holidays only.

Dress Code:
There is no uniform; students are however required to be dressed in neat and comfortable clothes. Girl’s hair, if long, needs to be tied up and boy’s hair needs to be kept short.

Term Dates:

These will coincide with the school terms as set out by the department.

Benefits of Royal Learning Academy Tutor Centre:

o Studies have shown that students who study at Tutor Centers tend to perform better academically overall than public learners.
o Teachers/Tutors can spend extra time with students on difficult concepts and move ahead after students have mastered a concept.
o Teachers/Tutors provide the student with the personal interaction that is not possible in large classrooms.
o Home Tutor Centers are advantageous to students who need some extra attention, whether it is due to a lack of prior teaching, a specific learning disability or trouble coping with the pressure and pace of mainstream academics.

FAQ:

o What is the Impak Curriculum?
     - Impak is a leading homeschooling provider with a strong national center network assisting the students.
     - Impak curriculum is in line with the National Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statement (CAPS).
     - Following the Impak curriculum allow students to go on to write the National Senior Certificate and continue on to University should they choose to.

o Should I register with the Department of Education?
     - Yes. The SA School Act requires parents to register their children for schooling at a Tutor Center. The registration must be done at the Provincial Department of Education.         
     - Forms are available at Royal Learning Academy and staff will assist the parents.


o How is the learning progress assessed?
     - Formal assessments are done in June and November.
     - November receives a report for the Grade-Level achieved.

o What is the language of Instruction?
     Instruction is done in both English and Afrikaans.

o How does the Education of the student progress after the Tutor Center?
     - Parents can continue to homeschool on their own.
     - Other Tutor Centers in the area.
     - Schooling can continue at mainstream schools - governmental schools or private schools.
