Welcome from The Founder

I am an Educator, wife and mother of three prematurely born children. My first-born was born and 30 weeks, my second at 32 weeks and my son at 35 weeks. With 3 premature born children I was confronted with the challenge and risk of possible long-term intellectual and developmental disabilities in my children. This challenge helped me to refocus my attention, as an educator, on the early and foundational development of children and led me to establish Royal Learning Academy. Today all three my children are above average in their developmental and intellectual abilities. My oldest daughter could read at age 5.

The dream that drives Royal Learning Academy is the intense desire to lay a solid educational foundation in every learner’s life. According to the National Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statement (CAPS) education comprises four phases:

  • Foundation Phase (Grade R – 3)
  • Intermediate Phase (Grade 4 – 6)
  • Senior Phase (Grade 7 – 9)
  • FET Phase (Grade 10 – 12)

We believe that the most important years of a child’s educational journey is the foundational phase (Grade R-3). If they aren’t thought the right foundations, thinking methods and principles they will struggle with the rest of their school career.

Our team of highly experienced, qualified and registered staff are experts in supporting learners in the foundational phase. In partnership with our parents, we ensure that our learners and their children are loved, empowered and ready for their life. We offer a caring and warm environment that supports young learners. Each child is given one to one teaching and is treated as an individual in order to make excellent progress with their learning and all round development.

According to brain researchers and neurologists at various institutions, including the Riggs Institute, early education shapes the architecture of our brains. From age 4-10, a child's brain is more than twice as active as an adult's, eventually petering out at age 16 (matching adult values).

Based on our improved understanding of the human mind, Royals Learning Academy adopts two key principles:

  1. The sooner we teach our children, the more developed the wiring of their brains. During these formative years, the cerebral cortex creates many neural pathways, which are strengthened by repetition but, if left undeveloped, are vulnerable to elimination. So through engaging and entertaining activities designed for repeat stimulation, the connections between your child's neurons will be enriched and stabilized.
  2. The more children are exposed to, the more they can handle (before stress is induced, of course).
Early childhood development is imperative to your child's mental, emotional and physical well-being. Royals Learning Academy is a here to help children realize their self worth, their love of learning and, ultimately, their full potential. With a solid foundation your child will seamlessly transition into higher grades.

Our Commitments

Royal Learning Academy maintains a commitment to address the cultural, economic, educational, environmental, health, and social needs of its community.

  • Our instruction will be differentiated and lead to the success of the whole student.
  • Our child-centered environment will be safe and positive to support student learning, growth, and development.
  • Our staff will create a supportive environment that promotes collaboration, reflection, and ongoing professional growth with a unified focus on student success.
  • Our school will engage the community to work together and share the responsibility of educating students through effective means of communication and engagement.
  • We will provide extra time, modify, and/or offer individual support for students in need.
  • We will commit to using formal and/or informal assessments to guide instruction and provide specific feedback.
  • We will keep our interactions positive and share student behavior plans with necessary instructional staff.
  • We will consistently use positive language to foster a culture of acceptance and leadership, holding our students accountable while celebrating student success.
  • We will support student learning by sharing strategies and ideas to benefit all students.
  • We will have a growth mindset and professional respect by being intentional learners.
  • We will use a variety of communication tools to communicate with parents and stakeholders of celebrations, curriculum, growth opportunities, and how they can support our students.

Vision Statement

We envision a world in which every child, regardless of background, becomes a healthy and productive adult.

Mission Statement

Our mission is to foster each child’s well-being as a foundation for academic and life success by drawing on the strengths of the child’s entire community including family, educators, medical and mental health providers.

Core Values

Our values represent a set of fundamental beliefs about how we should operate that dictate our daily behavior and the decisions we make, as an organization and as individual employees. We also seek to jointly promote these values with our children, families, and partners.

  • EXCELLENCE: We have audacious expectations for our children, our families, and ourselves, accompanied by realistic plans to achieve them.
  • GROWTH: As people, and as an organization, we always seek to grow, learn, and evolve.
  • COURAGE: We have the courage to make bold choices and question the status quo. We acknowledge and learn from failure.
  • COMMUNITY: We act as one community.
  • SOUL: We value and seek out meaning in our work and in the world. We aim to nurture the physical, emotional, spiritual and mental well-being of all members of our community.
